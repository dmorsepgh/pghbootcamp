Here is something that just clicked for me, and once you see it you cannot unsee it.

Imagine you hand a client a Mac mini M4. Brand new. Already set up. Their business tools, their workflows, their A.I. automations, all loaded and ready to go. They plug it in, and it just works. That is the delivery.

Now here is where it gets interesting.

That Mac mini is connected to a GitHub repository. Think of GitHub as a master copy of everything on that computer. Every file, every script, every workflow, all stored in the cloud and linked back to the machine sitting on their desk.

When I want to update something on their computer, I do not drive to their office. I do not remote in and fumble around with their settings. I make the change on my end, push it to GitHub, and within minutes, their Mac mini pulls the update and applies it automatically. It is like pushing an app update to someone's phone, except it is their entire business setup.

One change from my desk. Reflected on their machine. Instantly. From anywhere in the world.

This is not magic. It is just version control working the way it was designed to. But for a service business, the implications are huge. You could manage ten clients, fifty clients, and update all of them in a single push. Fix a problem once, fix it everywhere. Roll out a new workflow, and every client gets it at the same time.

The Mac mini becomes less of a computer and more of a managed service. You are not just selling hardware. You are selling an always-current, always-maintained A.I. system that you control and improve over time.

That is a product. That is a business.
