# The Speaking Business Plan
## How to Make Real Money in a Room with AI

---

## THE CORE TRUTH

A keynote speaker who gets $5,000 a talk needs to book 200 talks to make a million dollars.

A seminar operator who runs one room of 50 people at $1,000 a head makes $50,000 in a weekend.

Same room. Same topic. Different business model.

The goal is not to be a speaker. The goal is to own the room.

---

## THE THREE WAYS TO MAKE MONEY SPEAKING

### Tier 1 — Get Paid to Show Up
Someone else promotes, fills the room, and cuts you a check to speak.
- **Rate range:** $500–$10,000/talk (entry to mid-level)
- **Your cut:** Flat fee + sometimes back-of-room sales
- **Upside:** Zero marketing cost, zero risk
- **Downside:** You're the vendor. They own the relationship.

**Best for:** Building credibility, getting reps, finding what resonates.

---

### Tier 2 — Back of Room (BOR) Sales
You speak for free or low fee — but you pitch an offer at the end.
- **The model:** 60–90 minute presentation → close from the stage
- **Offer range:** $297–$3,000 product or event seat
- **Conversion rate:** 10–20% of a warm room if you're any good
- **Example:** 50 people in the room × 15% conversion × $997 = $7,477 from one talk
- **Upside:** You own the customer. That's where the real money is.
- **Downside:** You need to learn to close. It's a skill.

**Best for:** Generating leads, selling programs, building list.

---

### Tier 3 — Own the Seminar
You promote it. You fill it. You run it. Every dollar in the room is yours.
- **The model:** 1–2 day intensive, 20–100 seats, premium ticket price
- **Revenue:** Ticket sales + upsell at event (membership, coaching, mastermind)
- **Pittsburgh Boot Camp math:**
  - 20 in-person × $1,000 = $20,000 ticket revenue
  - Virtual × $99 = variable (even 50 virtual = $4,950)
  - Platinum conversions at event: 10 × $195/mo = $1,950/mo recurring
  - Total first-weekend potential: $25,000–$35,000+
- **Upside:** You own the room, the list, the relationship, the recurring revenue
- **Downside:** You have to fill the room. That's the whole job.

**Best for:** Serious income. This is the business.

## THE AI SPEAKING NICHE — WHY IT'S PERFECT RIGHT NOW

Every business owner, manager, and professional in America is scared of AI.

They don't understand it. They don't know if they need it. They don't know if it's going to replace them.

That fear is the market.

You walk into a room and you answer the question they've been too embarrassed to Google: *"What do I actually do about AI?"*

**The sweet spot audience:**
- Business owners 35–60
- Corporate teams (HR, marketing, operations)
- Professional associations (Realtors, accountants, contractors, HR directors)
- Chambers of commerce
- Community colleges and continuing education programs

None of these people are going to a tech conference. They need someone who sounds like them, thinks like them, and explains it in plain language.

That person is you.

## THE MONEY MAP — YEAR ONE

### Phase 1: Build the Reps (Months 1–3)
Goal: 10 free or low-fee talks. Learn what lands. Sharpen the close.

- Chambers of commerce (always looking for speakers)
- Rotary clubs, BNI chapters
- Local business associations
- Community college continuing ed programs

**Revenue target:** $0–$5,000 (maybe some BOR sales)
**Real goal:** Figure out what makes the room lean forward.

---

### Phase 2: Charge What You're Worth (Months 4–6)
Goal: $1,500–$5,000 per keynote. BOR sales on every talk.

- Corporate lunch-and-learns ($1,500–$3,000 flat)
- Association keynotes ($2,500–$5,000)
- BOR offer: Boot Camp virtual ticket or Platinum membership
- Every talk ends with: "If you want to go deeper, here's how."

**Revenue target:** $15,000–$30,000

---

### Phase 3: Run the Room (Month 6+)
Goal: 4 seminars per year. Own the event. Own the upsell.

- Pittsburgh Boot Camp = the anchor event
- Quarterly 1-day workshops (smaller, lower cost to run)
- Licensing: teach other people to run your seminar in their city (the Carter & Drake model)

**Revenue target:** $100,000–$200,000/year from events alone

## HOW TO GET BOOKED

### Inbound (let them come to you)
- YouTube builds authority → speaking requests come in organically
- Newsletter → subscribers become event promoters
- LinkedIn posts about AI → corporate HR/training departments find you

### Outbound (go get it)
- Email 10 chambers of commerce this week. Offer a free 30-min talk on AI.
- Call local community colleges. Ask about their continuing ed program.
- Identify 5 professional associations in Pittsburgh. They all have annual events.
- One email template, 50 organizations, 10 responses, 3 bookings. That's the math.

### The Pitch (what you say when you reach out)

*"I do a 45-minute presentation called 'AI Without the Hype' — a plain-language guide for business owners who want to understand what AI can actually do for their operation. No tech background required. I've presented this to [insert first group you speak to]. I'm available [dates]. Would this be a fit for your [conference/meeting/members]?"*

Short. Specific. Easy to say yes to.

---

## THE TALK (what you actually say)

Every talk, same three-part structure:

**Part 1 — The Problem (10 min)**
Everyone is confused about AI. Here's why. And here's why that confusion is costing you money.

**Part 2 — The System (25–35 min)**
Three things you can do this week. Live demos. Real output. No theory.
(This is where Demos 1–3 from Boot Camp live — prompt engineering, lead research, content multiplication)

**Part 3 — The Close (10 min)**
"Here's what the people who are winning with AI have in common. Here's how to be one of them. Here's the next step if you want to go further."

Then you make the offer.
