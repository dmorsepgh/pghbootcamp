## DEMO 1: "The Prompt That Pays"
**Time:** 20 minutes · Opening demo, right at 1:00 PM
**The Hook:** "Give me your worst business email. We're going to fix it live."

**How It Runs:**
1. Ask for a volunteer — someone with a real email they haven't sent yet. Proposal, follow-up, cold outreach, doesn't matter.
2. Read it aloud. Let the room feel how flat it is.
3. Paste it into Claude with a bad, lazy prompt: "Make this better."
4. Show the mediocre result.
5. Now write the real prompt — specific, with context, tone, objective, and constraints.
6. Show the new result side by side.

**The Punchline:** The difference between those two outputs is the difference between a closed deal and a deleted email. The only variable was how you asked.

**Why This Demo:** Every person in the room has written a bad email this week. This one hits home immediately. No technical knowledge required to feel the impact.

## DEMO 2: "The 10-Minute Lead Research Machine"
**Time:** 25 minutes
**The Hook:** "By the time you finish your coffee, AI has researched your next 20 prospects."

**How It Runs:**
1. Start with a real list — 5 company names, typed live, no prep.
2. Build a simple prompt chain: company name → AI researches → generates personalized outreach for each.
3. Run it. Show the output populating in real time.
4. Pick one result. Read the personalized outreach aloud.
5. Ask the room: "How long would that have taken your VA?"

**The Punchline:** You just did in 10 minutes what an intern would spend half a day on. And it knew which golf course their CEO mentioned on LinkedIn.

**Why This Demo:** Shows AI as an employee, not a toy. Agency owners and consultants feel this directly. The ROI math is instant.

## DEMO 3: "The Content Multiplication Engine"
**Time:** 15 minutes
**The Hook:** "One idea. Ten pieces of content. Five minutes. Watch."

**How It Runs:**
1. Take one concept from Joanna Doven's morning keynote — a real idea from earlier in the day.
2. Run it through a single prompt chain:
   - LinkedIn post
   - Twitter/X thread (5 tweets)
   - Email newsletter intro
   - YouTube video title + hook line
   - Short-form video script (60 seconds)
3. Show all output side by side.
4. Ask: "Who here is posting once a week and running out of ideas?"

**The Punchline:** You've been mining a vein of gold with a teaspoon. This is a backhoe.

**Why This Demo:** Content is the universal pain point. Doesn't matter if you're a consultant, agency, or operator — everyone needs more of it. This demo makes the room feel the leverage immediately.

## DEMO 4: "Build Your AI Employee"
**Time:** 60–75 minutes (the main Implementation Block)
**The Hook:** "You're going to build something in the next hour that will save you 5 hours a week. And you're going to build it yourself."

**How It Runs:**
1. Everyone opens their laptop. Virtual attendees follow along.
2. Carter & Drake walk the room through building ONE complete, usable workflow:
   - **Recommended build:** AI-powered client intake + onboarding emailsequence generator
   - Input: new client name, project type, scope
   - Output: onboarding email, first-week checklist, kickoff agenda, welcome message
3. Walk through it step by step. Pause for questions. Help stragglers.
4. At the 60-minute mark: "Save it. Test it with a real client name."
5. Feed directly into the 2:30 PM Show & Tell — everyone demos what they built.

**The Punchline at Show & Tell:** The room just built something real. Not a prototype. A thing they can use tomorrow. That's the whole point of the Boot Camp.

**Why This Demo:** This IS the event. Demos 1–3 are warm-up. Demo 4 is the proof of concept. Every person walks out with something functional and the confidence that they can build more.

## DEMO 5: "VS Code + Claude — Live Build"
**Time:** 12 minutes · Kicks off the Implementation Block
**The Hook:** "Who has a business? You're about to have a website."

**How It Runs:**
1. VS Code open on screen. Blank folder. Terminal visible.
2. Ask the room for a volunteer — get their business name, what they do, one goal.
3. Open terminal. Type `claude`. One prompt, spoken out loud slowly.
4. The room watches Claude write the entire website in real time.
5. `open index.html` — browser opens, full screen.
6. Turn to the volunteer: "Is that your business?"

**The Punchline:** They didn't see code. They saw a conversation. And at the end of the conversation, there was a website.

**Full script:** `demos/vscode-claude-demo/DEMO-SCRIPT.md`

**Why This Demo:** This is the one that changes the room. Everyone else has been talking about AI. This is AI doing something, on screen, for someone in the room, in under 12 minutes. The insight isn't "how do I code." It's "I don't have to."
